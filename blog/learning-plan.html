Long-Term Goal / Career Pathway

My long-term goal is to become a full-stack developer and eventually break into game development. I want a career where I can build cool, useful or fun digital experiences, ideally something creative, like games or interactive tools. I also want to set myself up to work remotely or even freelance so I can spend more time with my whānau, while doing something I enjoy.

Strengths & Limitations as a Learner

Strengths:

  • I learn best by working hands-on. Real world examples stick with me more than theory alone.
  • I am curious and motivated. If something interests me, I'll chase it down until I figure it out.
  • I've already started building things like websites, scripts, little games and I've been teaching myself for a while.
  • I like challenges and I enjoy breaking problems down to find solutions.
  • If I don't understand something, I'm not afraid to ask questions.
  • I don't care what people think of me, respectfully.

Limitations:

  • I sometimes doubt myself or overthink.
  • I can get overwhelmed if too many tasks stack up without structure.
  • I tend to work independently, so I'm still getting used to working and sharing openly in a group setting.
  • I'm juggling learning with being a father. Time management will be key for me.
  • I don't think I'm a good communicator. I struggle to explain things or can't find the right words. (It's like my brain is firing faster than I can speak).
  • Time delegation and distraction when working on my personal projects.

Non-Technical / Human Skills I Want to Develop

  • Clear and confident communication, especially when explaining my thinking or giving feedback.
  • How to ask the right questions.
  • Working effectively in a team. Listening, collaborating, and sharing ideas.
  • Time management and learning how to plan smarter, not just harder.
  • Staying calm and focused when things don't go to plan.

Workload & Time Management Commitments

How I'll Work Productively and Safely:

  • I'll treat Dev Academy like a job, show up, be present, and do the mahi.
  • I'll respect the space, the kaupapa, and everyone's time.
  • I'll make an effort to communicate clearly and support others where I can.
  • Using Toggl to track my time.

When Things Get Tough:

  • I'll take a break, go outside, reset instead of powering through blindly.
  • I'll talk to the team instead of isolating myself.
  • I'll check in with myself and my schedule, then reprioritise tasks to lighten the mental load.

How and When I'll Seek Help:

  • If I'm stuck for more than 20-30 mins and have already tried a few things, I'll reach out and ask a facilitator or another team member.
  • I'll be specific when asking for help. Show what I've tried and where I'm blocked.
  • I won't be afraid to speak up, even if it feels awkward. We're all here to learn.

What I Expect From the Facilitation Team

  • Honest, clear feedback, not just what I did wrong, but how I can grow from it.
  • Support if I'm stuck, not just give me the answers, but help guide me back on track.
  • Respect and understanding that I'm balancing a lot outside the course too.

My Scheduling / Block-Out Times

  • I'm a father and work full time driving a truck, so I'm very strict with not being on my phone. Every work day is different so it's hard to know when I'll be stopped to be able to check my phone.
  • My best focused hours are:
    • 7-10:30pm weekdays. Can be off and on from 5pm
    • Thursdays I finish early to pick the kids up, so I'm on and off from 3.30pm onwards. Full time 7pm onwards
    • I can check in most evenings after 7pm if needed
    • Samoa trip: Sept 29 - Oct 7
    • Maybe away over Xmas/New Years (not confirmed)
  • If anything unexpected comes up (family emergency, sick kids, etc), I'll communicate ASAP and work around it professionally.

Final Words

I'm fully in. I want to grow, learn, and challenge myself, not just to “pass” this bootcamp, but to walk away a better version of myself, with skills I can be proud of. I'll commit to showing up, being real, and giving it my all.
