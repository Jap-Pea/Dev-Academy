Three things I learnt about myself & ego

  • My ego makes me stubborn sometimes. I'll grind solo too long instead of asking for help.
  • I get frustrated when I don't pick things up fast, that's just ego wanting instant results.
  • When I check my ego, I enjoy learning more because I focus on progress, not perfection.

Role of values, empathy, and self-awareness

  • Values reminds me how I want to show up. When I'm debugging, that means sticking it out instead of giving up. When Im stuck, it means being straight up about it instead of pretending Ive got it.
  • Empathy is about stepping into someone else's shoes, whether it's the user trying to click through my site, or a teammate explaining their idea. It smooths out the bumps when we're working together.
  • Self-awareness is important. Knowing when I'm stuck, when I'm tired, or when I need to try a different approach stops me from spiralling.

What surprised me

I thought Human Skills were a bit "extra", but they directly affect how fast I learn, how I debug, and how to work and communicate in group projects.

Most challenging parts

Reflection. Slowing down to look at my process felt awkward at first, but I could see how much I learnt just by stopping and looking back at my process.

Why a dev bootcamp teaches Human Skills

Real dev work is teamwork, feedback, stress management, and communication on top of code. Human Skills make you employable and make teams effective.

Was this a waste of time?

Nah. More coding alone isn't the answer. These skills give me tools to stay calm, to work with others, and to actually enjoy the journey. So in the long run, this time will save me from wasting way more time later.
