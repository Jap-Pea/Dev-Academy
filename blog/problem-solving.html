Blocked on a Simple Problem

I got stuck on what looked like a really small problem (looping through an array to build a gradebook object) I kept trying things like gradebook.students or gradebook[students], thinking it made sense, but it never worked.

To get through it, I slowed down and tried to break down the instructions into something that I could comprehend and would 'click'. Then I used console.log to check each step and even talked it out to myself (rubber ducky method).

At the time I felt frustrated and lost. I couldn't comprehend the instructions. I learned was to slow down, break the problem into smaller steps, and trust the process.

Elegantly Solved a Problem

Another task was to calculate an average. Instead of overcomplicating it, I just thought, add the numbers together and divide by how many there are. I wrote a simple loop and it worked first try.

It felt good to get something right in such a clean way. I learned that not every problem needs a clever or complex solution, sometimes the simplest approach is the best.

Confidence with Problem-Solving Techniques

  • Pseudocode - Helpful when I take the time, but I don't always remember to do it.
  • Trying something - I do this often, but I need to reflect more so I don't keep looping back over the same mistakes.
  • Rubber ducky method - Works better than I expected, saying things out loud often shows me mistakes.
  • Reading error messages - I'm building confidence here. They usually tell me the answer if I actually read them properly.
  • Console.logging - My go-to tool. It helps me see what's really happening in my code.
  • Googling - I'm confident, but learning to be more specific with my search terms (like “JavaScript object key by variable” instead of just “JavaScript help lol”).
  • Asking peers for help - I never really ask my peers for help, I like cracking problems on my own, even if it takes longer. It is something I know I need to get better at.
  • Asking coaches for help - I hardly ever ask for help, but I know asking could save me time and give me new perspectives.
  • Reflection - Writing things like this shows me what's working and what I need to improve.

Reluctant to Ask for Help

Honestly, I don't really ask for help much with everything. I've got this stubborn streak where I want to sort things out on my own, even if it takes longer.

It works sometimes, but I know I could save time (and stress) if I asked sooner. That's something I want to get better at but still keeping my independence and not wasting hours stuck on little things.
